Dr. Deepak Gupta

Postdoctoral Researcher · Engineering Science & Mechanics
Pennsylvania State University

I engineer living tissues — using 3D bioprinting, biomaterials, and precision manufacturing to build vascularized scaffolds that bridge lab science and clinical translation. My work spans bone reconstruction, organ-on-a-chip models, and the first-ever "printing with air" technology published in Nature.

Research Focus

Research Interests

Bridging materials science, biology, and engineering to create functional living constructs for regenerative medicine and disease modelling.

3D & 4D Bioprinting
Multi-arm and droplet-based bioprinters; embedded printing; 6-axis robotic arm bioprinting for intraoperative use.
Tissue Engineering
Bone, vascular, corneal, and muscle tissue fabrication; hierarchical scaffold design inspired by natural ECM architecture.
Biomaterials
Hydrogels, polymers, ceramics, and composites; stimuli-responsive microgels; bioinks with precise rheological properties.
Vascularization Strategies
"3D Printing of Air" — hollow channel networks; perfusable tissue constructs; microgel-based angiogenesis models.
Disease Modelling
Organ-on-a-chip platforms; tumour microenvironment models; in-vitro tissue models for drug testing.
Microfluidics & Microgels
High-throughput microgel fabrication; co-axial jetting; ion-responsive and shape-morphing microgel systems.

Active Research

Current Projects

Lab projects at the intersection of advanced biofabrication, biomaterials, and translational medicine.

01 In Review · Nature

3D Printing of Air

Pioneering a paradigm shift by using air as a bioink — directly printing hollow, perfusable channel networks within soft bioprinted constructs. Enables fabrication of vascularized artificial tissues for disease modelling and regenerative therapy without biodegradable sacrificial materials.

Vascularization Bioink Air Printing Perfusable Tissue
Funding: NIH $3M (2024–2028) · Patent Filed · Lead idea conception & full proposal writing
02 Ongoing

4D Bioprinting of Microgel-based Vascularized Muscle

Developing bioactive self-actuating microgel scaffolds that undergo time-dependent (4D) shape change after printing. Targets faster vascularization of muscle tissue constructs using stimuli-responsive hydrogel systems with embedded growth factors.

4D Bioprinting Microgels Muscle Tissue Stimuli-responsive
Funding: MRI $50K (2025–2027) · Full proposal writing
03 Published

Intraoperative Robotic Arm Bioprinting

Deployed a 6-axis EPSON robotic arm for in-situ bioprinting directly into craniomaxillofacial defects in rat and sheep models. Published in Small Science. Demonstrates clinical feasibility of direct-to-wound bioprinting during surgery.

Robotic Arm In-situ Bioprinting Bone Reconstruction Translational
Collaborator: Dr. Dino Ravnic, Hershey Medical Centre
04 Ongoing

High-throughput Microgel Platforms

Engineering air-assisted co-axial jetting systems for scalable production of uniform microgels. Applications span cell encapsulation, granular bioinks for 3D printing, and modular vascularized tissue assembly. Systematic ion-responsive crosslinking for programmable mechanics.

Microgels Co-axial Jetting Cell Encapsulation Granular Bioink
Related patents: Microgel Composition (Filed)
05 In Review

AI-guided Bioprinting Optimization

Collaborating with machine learning experts at Federal Institute of São Paulo to build AI-driven process controllers for 3D bioprinting. Targets real-time optimization of print fidelity, strand geometry, and cell viability from sensor feedback loops.

Artificial Intelligence Process Control Print Fidelity Collaboration
Collaborator: J. Robazzi & A. Dias, Federal Institute of São Paulo, Brazil
06 Ongoing

Bone Scaffold Doctoral Research Legacy

Foundational work on natural-bone-inspired 3D-printed hierarchical scaffolds for orthopaedic and craniomaxillofacial reconstruction. Includes cryogenic gel printing, PCL frameworks, and surface treatment. Thesis awarded the Naik & Rastogi Excellence in Ph.D. Thesis Award 2022.

Bone Graft Hierarchical Scaffold PCL Hydrogel
Grants: DST · Tata Centre for Tech & Design (IIT Bombay)
